## 1. Welcome — what you own

You don't own "a website". You own a **complete, professional sales channel** for
Sunflour Bakery — a mobile-first ordering platform that works for you 24 hours a
day, takes orders while you sleep, and presents your bakery the way a serious brand
deserves to be seen.

This is an **asset**, not a rented page. It is yours: your brand, your menu, your
prices, your customers, and your data — with **no per-order commissions** to a
third-party marketplace eating into your margins.

### What it does for your customers

- Browse your official menu with photos, and **search** with live suggestions.
- Add items to a cart and choose **pickup** or **delivery**.
- See the **exact delivery fee before they pay** — no surprises, no disputes.
- Get clear **Moniepoint bank-transfer** instructions.
- Send **payment proof on WhatsApp** in one tap.
- View and download a proper **invoice** anytime.
- Leave a **review** — which you approve before it ever appears.

### What it does for your business

| The value | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| **Your own storefront, 24/7** | Customers order anytime, from any phone — you're open even when the shop is closed. |
| **You keep your margins** | No marketplace commissions. Every naira of the sale is yours. |
| **A professional first impression** | A clean, branded, fast site builds instant trust and makes Sunflour look established and serious. |
| **Honest money handling** | The platform never claims a payment is confirmed until *you* verify it — protecting your cash and your word. |
| **Full operational control** | One private Admin panel runs products, prices, images, delivery zones, payments, orders, reviews, emails, your team, and settings. |
| **You own the relationship** | Customer details, order history, and reviews live in *your* system — not a third party's. |
| **Built to be found** | Search-engine ready, so new customers can discover Sunflour on Google. |

### The one principle behind everything

**The golden rule of this platform: it never tells a customer that payment is
confirmed until _you_ confirm it.** This single discipline protects your money,
keeps your promises honest, and is the reason customers will trust ordering from you
again and again. Everything in the platform is built around that trust.

> In short: this is the engine that turns Sunflour from a bakery people *visit*
> into a bakery people can *order from, anywhere, with confidence.*

## 3. The customer journey

Understanding what your customer sees helps you support them.

1. **Home** — a warm, branded landing page with featured products.
2. **Menu** — they browse products (with photos) or switch to a **quick price
   list**, and can **search** with live suggestions.
3. **Product page** — full details, price, and "add to cart".
4. **Cart** — they review items and pick **pickup** or **delivery**.
5. **Checkout** — they enter details; the platform calculates the **exact total**,
   including any delivery fee and evening surcharge.
6. **Payment instructions** — they see your **Moniepoint** transfer details and a
   clear note that payment is *pending* until you confirm it.
7. **WhatsApp proof** — they tap a button to send you their payment proof.
8. **Invoice** — they can view/download their invoice anytime.
9. **Updates** — they receive emails as the order progresses (if email is on).

A first-time visitor also sees a short **welcome message** that appears once and
stays quiet for 7 days.

## 4. Managing your menu

### 4.1 Categories
Create categories (e.g. *Cakes*, *Bread*, *Pastries*) under **Admin → Categories**.
Categories control how products are grouped on the menu. Order matters — they
appear in the sort order you set.

### 4.2 Products
Under **Admin → Products** you can:

- Add a product with a **name, description, category, and price**.
- Add **variants** (e.g. sizes) with their own prices.
- Upload **product images** (the first image is the main photo).
- Set a product as **popular** or **featured** (these surface on the home page).
- Mark a product **available / unavailable** without deleting it.

> **Prices are in Naira.** Always double-check a price before saving — the price
> you set is the price customers pay. The platform never lets the customer's
> device change the price; the total is always recalculated on the server.

### 4.3 Drafts (save and finish later)
If you start adding a product but aren't ready to publish, it can be saved as a
**draft** and finished later. Drafts are not visible to customers.

### 4.4 Images — keep them clean
Use clear, well-lit, square-ish photos. Good photos are the single biggest driver
of appetite and trust. Avoid blurry or dark images.

### 4.5 The tabular menu
The **Tabular menu** is a fast, scannable price list (name, photo, price). It's a
**reference** for browsing — when a customer checks out, the platform always uses
your live product data and prices, never the table.

## 5. Orders and the order lifecycle

Every order moves through clear stages. You drive it forward from **Admin →
Orders**.

| Order status | Meaning | Your action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Pending payment** | Order placed, not paid yet | Wait for payment proof |
| **Payment under review** | Customer sent proof | Verify the transfer |
| **Payment confirmed** | You confirmed the money arrived | Start preparing |
| **Preparing** | You're making it | Bake / pack |
| **Ready for pickup** | Pickup order is ready | Tell the customer |
| **Out for delivery** | On the way | Deliver |
| **Delivered** | Completed | Done ✅ |
| **Cancelled / Rejected** | Order stopped | Explain to the customer |

Two important points:

- **The platform records every change** with a timestamp, so you always have a
  trail of what happened and when.
- **Invalid jumps are blocked.** You can't mark something "delivered" before it's
  paid and prepared. This keeps operations honest.

Payment has its own state too: *Unpaid → Proof sent on WhatsApp → Under review →
Confirmed* (or *Rejected*).

## 6. Getting paid (Moniepoint)

Sunflour uses **manual Moniepoint bank transfer** in this version. Here's the flow:

1. Customer checks out and sees your **Moniepoint account details** (managed under
   **Admin → Payment**).
2. Customer transfers the money and **sends proof on WhatsApp**.
3. You **check your Moniepoint app** to confirm the money actually arrived.
4. In **Admin → Orders**, you mark the payment **Confirmed** (or **Rejected** if it
   didn't arrive or was wrong).
5. Only then does the order move forward.

> ⚠️ **Never confirm payment you haven't actually received.** The WhatsApp proof is
> a *claim*, not proof of funds. Always verify in your Moniepoint app first.
> Confirming and rejecting are both recorded in your audit log.

To change the bank details customers see, update **Admin → Payment**. Existing
invoices keep the details they were created with, so old orders stay accurate.

## 7. Delivery, pickup and fees

Under **Admin → Delivery**:

- **Delivery zones** — create the areas you deliver to, each with its **base fee**.
- **Pickup** is always **free** (₦0) and never carries a surcharge.
- **Evening surcharge** — delivery orders placed **from 6:00 PM** get an extra
  **₦500**, unless you disable the rule. Pickup is never surcharged.

The customer always sees the delivery fee **before** paying — no surprises.

> You can edit zones and fees anytime. Changes apply to **new** orders; past
> invoices are frozen with the fees that applied when they were placed.

## 8. Reviews

- Customers can submit reviews from the **Reviews** page.
- Every review arrives as **Pending** — it is **not public** until you approve it.
- Under **Admin → Reviews**, you **approve** or **reject** each one.
- Approving/rejecting is recorded in your audit log.

This protects your brand from spam and abuse while still showing genuine, approved
feedback.

## 9. Emails your customers receive

The platform can send these transactional emails (no marketing/spam):

| Email | When it sends |
| --- | --- |
| **Order confirmation** | After an order is placed |
| **Purchase invoice** | With the order's invoice |
| **Order status update** | When you advance the order |
| **New order alert** | To your staff inboxes, on every new order |
| **Password reset** | When an admin resets their password |
| **Appreciation** | After a delivery is completed |

How email works (important):

- Emails are **queued** and sent by a small scheduled job every few minutes. This
  means an email may arrive a couple of minutes after the action — that's normal.
- Email is **deliberately separate from ordering**: if email ever has a problem,
  **orders still work perfectly**. Customers can always see their invoice on the
  site.
- For email to send, a few settings must be configured (see
  [Section 14](#14-going-live-checklist)). You can check what was sent or is
  waiting under **Admin → Email**.

## 10. Your team and access

### 10.1 Roles
Each staff member has a role that controls what they can do:

| Role | Can do |
| --- | --- |
| **Super admin** | Everything, including managing other admins and settings |
| **Moderator** | Day-to-day orders, payment review, availability, reviews |
| **Attendant** | Order operations allowed for their role |
| **Media manager** | Product photos and content (no payment/admin powers) |
| **Customer** | The normal public + their own account |

Permissions are enforced on the server, so a staff member can never do more than
their role allows — even if they try.

### 10.2 Adding a staff member (registration codes)
New admins create their own account using a **role-specific code**:

1. As a **Super admin**, open **Admin → Users** to see the **current codes** (there
   is one per role).
2. Share the correct code with the new staff member **privately**.
3. They go to `/admin-register`, choose their role, and enter the code to create
   their account.

Key facts about codes:

- Codes **change automatically every week** — old codes stop working. This is a
  security feature.
- You can **rotate them immediately** from **Admin → Users** if a code leaks.
- Codes only let someone *register* — they are **not** login passwords.

### 10.3 Removing access
Remove or deactivate a staff member from **Admin → Users**. Do this promptly when
someone leaves.

## 11. Security and account recovery

### 11.1 Everyday security habits
- Use **strong, unique passwords** for every admin.
- Share registration codes **privately** (never post them publicly).
- Remove staff access as soon as someone leaves.
- Keep your domain, Vercel, Resend, and database logins safe.

### 11.2 The two "master keys" — back these up
Store these somewhere safe and offline (a password manager):

1. **Database connection** (`DATABASE_URL`).
2. **Admin registration code secret** (`ADMIN_REGISTRATION_CODE_SECRET`).

Together, these let your developer recover access in an emergency.

### 11.3 "I forgot my password and email isn't working"
You can still recover:

1. Your developer retrieves the **current Super-admin registration code** (from
   Admin → Users while still signed in, or by running the provided script).
2. Register a **new Super-admin** at `/admin-register` with that code and a new
   email — you're back in.
3. Clean up the old account afterwards.

### 11.4 "We lost the registration code secret"
No problem: set a **new** secret value and redeploy. From that moment the codes are
generated from the new secret, the current codes appear in Admin → Users, and you
can register again. (Anyone holding old codes loses access — which is exactly what
you want.) This is why backing up the secret matters: losing it is recoverable, but
it briefly invalidates everyone's codes.

> These recovery steps are technical — your developer/Bespoke Technologies can run
> them quickly. The important thing for you is to **keep the two master keys
> backed up**.

## 13. Getting found on Google (SEO)

The platform is built to rank well, but it needs a couple of one-time setup steps:

- It automatically produces a **sitemap** (a map of your pages, including every
  product) and a **robots** file that tells search engines what to index.
- Each product page has its own **title, description, and share image**.
- Your bakery's details power **rich results** (Google can show your name, area,
  and contact).

To finish SEO setup:

1. Make sure your live address is set as the **canonical domain** (your developer
   sets `NEXT_PUBLIC_APP_URL`).
2. Register the site in **Google Search Console** and **submit your sitemap**
   (`/sitemap.xml`).
3. Keep product names and descriptions clear and specific — that's what people
   search for.

A simple daily rhythm:

1. **Check Orders** — confirm payments you've actually received; advance orders.

3. **Update statuses** — keep customers informed (ready / out for delivery /

- Confirm new-order alert emails are arriving.

## 16. Troubleshooting & FAQ

**A customer says they paid but the order didn't move.**
Payment is confirmed manually. Check your Moniepoint app; if the money arrived,
mark the payment **Confirmed** in **Admin → Orders**.

**Emails aren't arriving.**
Orders still work regardless. Check **Admin → Email** to see if messages are
queued, and confirm email settings/domain verification with your developer. Also
ask customers to check spam.

**A price looks wrong to a customer.**
The price they pay is always the one in **Admin → Products** — the platform
recalculates totals on the server. Update the product and the new price applies to
new orders.

**A staff member can't access something.**
That's their **role** limiting them by design. A Super-admin can adjust roles in
**Admin → Users**.

**A registration code "doesn't work".**
Codes rotate weekly — share the **current** code from **Admin → Users**. Make sure
they picked the matching **role**.

**I'm locked out.**
See [Section 11.3](#11-security-and-account-recovery). Keep two Super-admins to
avoid this.

## 17. Glossary

- **Admin panel** — the private management area at `/admin`.
- **Variant** — a version of a product (e.g. a size) with its own price.
- **Draft** — an unfinished product not shown to customers.
- **Order status** — where an order is in its journey.
- **Payment status** — whether money has been received and confirmed.
- **Surcharge** — the extra ₦500 on evening deliveries.
- **Snapshot** — the frozen copy of details on an invoice, so old orders never
  change when you update prices or settings later.
- **Audit log** — the history of important admin actions.
- **Registration code** — a weekly code used to create a new admin account.
- **Sitemap** — the list of pages search engines use to find your content.
